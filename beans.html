About Beans

Beans are a very important food crop in Kenya. They are a good source of protein and are eaten by millions of families every day. Beans also improve soil by adding nitrogen naturally — making them great to grow alongside maize.

📅 When to Plant
  • Long rains: March – April
  • Short rains: October – November
  • Plant when the soil is warm and moist
  • Ready for harvest in about 60 to 90 days

🌱 Planting Stages
  1. Land Preparation – Clear weeds and dig the soil well
  2. Planting – Plant seeds 3–5cm deep; 30cm apart in rows
  3. Germination – Seeds sprout in 4–6 days
  4. Seedling Stage – First leaves appear in 1–2 weeks
  5. Vegetative Stage – Plant grows more leaves and branches
  6. Flowering – White or purple flowers appear around week 5
  7. Pod Formation – Flowers turn into bean pods
  8. Harvest – Pods turn yellow/dry; ready at 2–3 months

🦠 Common Diseases
  • Bean Rust – Orange-brown spots on leaves; spreads in wet weather
  • Angular Leaf Spot – Brown angular patches on leaves
  • Bean Common Mosaic Virus – Yellow mosaic pattern on leaves
  • Root Rot – Roots turn brown and plant wilts
Prevention: Use disease-resistant varieties and avoid waterlogging. 		🐛 Common Pests
  • Bean Fly – Larvae damage stem base; plant wilts early
  • Aphids – Suck sap and spread viruses
  • Thrips – Small insects that damage flowers
  • Bruchids (Bean Weevil) – Attack stored beans
Control: Plant early, use pesticides, and store beans well.

⭐ Why Beans are Important
  • Very good source of protein — important for body growth
  • Affordable food for low-income families
  • Improves soil fertility by fixing nitrogen
  • Can be grown together with maize to save space
  • Easy to store and sell at the local market
