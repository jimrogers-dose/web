About Tea

Tea is Kenya's most important cash crop and one of its biggest exports. Kenya is the third largest tea producer in the world. Tea is grown mainly in the highlands of Kericho, Nandi, Kisii, and Muranga. It provides income for over 600,000 smallholder farmers.

📅 When to Plant
  • Best planted at the start of the long rains: March – April
  • Grows in areas with 1200mm–2800mm of rainfall per year
  • Tea is a perennial crop — it does not need replanting each year
  • First harvest is after 3–5 years; then harvested every 2 weeks
  • A tea bush can produce for over 50 years

🌱 Planting Stages
  1. Nursery – Cuttings are taken from good mother bushes and grown in a nursery for 6–12 months
  2. Land Preparation – Land is cleared and deep holes (60cm) are dug with compost
  3. Transplanting – Seedlings are planted in rows 1.2m apart
  4. Establishment – Young bushes grow for 1–2 years; regular pruning begins
  5. Tipping – The top bud is removed to encourage bushy growth
  6. Bush Formation – Plant forms a wide flat shape for easy plucking
  7. First Plucking – Begins after 3–5 years; only the top two leaves and a bud are picked
  8. Regular Harvest – Plucked every 7–14 days for many decades

🦠 Common Diseases
  • Tea Blister Blight – Glass-like blisters on young leaves; caused by fungus
  • Brown Blight – Brown patches on leaves; spreads in wet weather
  • Die-Back – Branches dry up and die; caused by poor pruning or fungus
  • Root Rot – Roots decay; plant wilts and dies slowly
Prevention: Prune well, spray copper-based fungicides, drain soil properly. 		🐛 Common Pests
  • Tea Mosquito Bug – Sucks sap from young shoots; causes brown tips
  • Red Spider Mite – Causes bronze discolouration of leaves
  • Thrips – Small insects that damage young buds
  • Scale Insects – Attach to stems and suck plant sap
Control: Use miticides, regular pruning, and avoid water stress.

⭐ Why Tea is Important
  • Kenya's number one export crop — earns billions of shillings
  • Provides steady income because it is harvested every 2 weeks
  • Supports the livelihoods of over 600,000 smallholder farmers
  • Grown by KTDA (Kenya Tea Development Agency) which helps small farmers
  • Kenya tea is exported to Pakistan, Egypt, UK and many other countries
