About Maize

Maize (also called corn) is one of the most important food crops in Kenya and across Africa. It is used to make ugali, porridge, and animal feed. It grows well in many parts of Kenya.


📅 When to Plant
  • Long rains season: March – April
  • Short rains season: October – November
  • Best planted when soil is moist after the first rains
  • Takes about 3 to 4 months to be ready for harvest

🌱 Planting Stages
  1. Soil Preparation – Plough and dig the land to loosen the soil
  2. Planting – Plant seeds 5cm deep, spacing 75cm between rows and 25cm between plants
  3. Germination – Seeds sprout after 5–7 days
  4. Seedling Stage – Young plants grow leaves in the first 3 weeks
  5. Vegetative Stage – Plant grows tall with many leaves (weeks 3–8)
  6. Tasseling & Silking – Flowers appear at the top (week 8–10)
  7. Grain Filling – Cobs develop and fill with grain
  8. Maturity & Harvest – Cobs are dry and ready at 3–4 months

🦠 Common Diseases
  • Maize Streak Virus – Yellow streaks on leaves; spread by insects
  • Grey Leaf Spot – Grey-brown spots on leaves; caused by fungi
  • Stalk Rot – Stem rots and plant falls over
  • Smut – Black powdery growth on the cob
Prevention: Use certified seeds and crop rotation. 🐛 Common Pests
  • Fall Armyworm – Eats leaves; very destructive
  • Maize Stalk Borer – Bores into the stem; causes wilting
  • Aphids – Small insects that suck sap from leaves
  • Weevils – Attack stored maize grain
Control: Use pesticides and store grain in clean dry stores.
⭐ Why Maize is Important
  • It is the main staple food in Kenya — used to make ugali
  • Provides income for many smallholder farmers
  • Used as animal feed for chicken, cattle and pigs
  • Can be processed into flour, oil and starch
  • Grows in many different climates and altitudes
