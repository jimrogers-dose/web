About Tomato



Tomato is one of the most popular vegetables grown in Kenya. It is grown both for home use and for selling at the market. Tomatoes need proper care because they are easily affected by diseases. Farmers in Kirinyaga, Meru, and Rift Valley grow large amounts of tomatoes.


📅 When to Plant
  • Can be planted in both rainy seasons
  • Best planted: February–March or August–September
  • With irrigation, tomatoes can be grown all year round
  • Takes about 60–80 days from transplanting to harvest

🌱 Planting Stages
  1. Nursery – Seeds are planted in a seedbed first; takes 3–4 weeks
  2. Land Preparation – Main field is dug and mixed with compost
  3. Transplanting – Seedlings moved to the main field at 15–20cm tall
  4. Staking – Sticks are put next to plants to support them as they grow
  5. Vegetative Growth – Plant grows tall with many leaves
  6. Flowering – Yellow flowers appear
  7. Fruit Formation – Green tomatoes begin to form
  8. Ripening & Harvest – Tomatoes turn red; harvest begins at 2 months

🦠 Common Diseases
  • Early Blight – Brown spots with rings on older leaves
  • Late Blight – Dark wet patches on leaves; very dangerous
  • Fusarium Wilt – Plant wilts suddenly; stems turn brown inside
  • Tomato Mosaic Virus – Leaves turn yellow and curl
Prevention: Spray fungicides, avoid overwatering, rotate crops. 🐛 Common Pests
  • Whiteflies – Tiny white insects under leaves; spread viruses
  • Red Spider Mites – Cause yellow spots; worse in dry weather
  • Tomato Fruit Borer – Caterpillar that eats into the fruit
  • Aphids – Cluster on young shoots and spread disease
Control: Use insecticides and remove infected plants quickly.

⭐ Why Tomato is Important

  • One of the most profitable vegetables for small farmers
  • Rich in Vitamin C and antioxidants — good for health
  • Used in cooking every day in most Kenyan homes
  • Can be sold fresh, processed into sauce or canned
  • Short growing period means quick income for farmers
