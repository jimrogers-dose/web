About Potato The Irish potato is one of Kenya's most important food and cash crops. It grows best in cool highland areas like Nyandarua, Meru, and Elgeyo-Marakwet. Potato is eaten boiled, fried, or mashed, and gives farmers good income.


📅 When to Plant

  • Long rains: March – April
  • Short rains: October – November
  • Grows best in cool highlands (1500m – 3000m above sea level)
  • Ready for harvest in about 3 months


🌱 Planting Stages
  1. Seed Selection – Choose healthy certified seed potatoes (not just any potato)
  2. Land Preparation – Dig soil deep (30cm); add fertiliser or compost
  3. Planting – Place seed potato in furrow 10cm deep; 30cm apart
  4. Germination – Shoots appear above soil in 2–3 weeks
  5. Hilling Up – Mound soil around base of plants as they grow
  6. Vegetative Stage – Plant grows full leaves; tubers form underground
  7. Flowering – White or purple flowers appear (optional sign of maturity)
  8. Harvest – Dig up potatoes when leaves turn yellow at 3 months

🦠 Common Diseases
  • Late Blight – The most dangerous disease; dark wet spots on leaves and tubers
  • Early Blight – Brown spots with yellow rings on leaves
  • Bacterial Wilt – Plant wilts suddenly; inside of stem is slimy brown
  • Common Scab – Rough corky patches on the potato skin
Prevention: Use certified seeds, spray fungicides, avoid waterlogging. 🐛 Common Pests
  • Potato Tuber Moth – Caterpillar tunnels into tubers
  • Aphids – Spread viruses; found under leaves
  • Cut Worms – Cut young plants at the base at night
  • Nematodes – Tiny worms in soil that damage roots
Control: Rotate crops, use pesticides, plant certified seeds.
⭐ Why Potato is Important
  • One of the highest-yielding crops per hectare (up to 15 tonnes)
  • Major source of income for highland farmers in Kenya
  • Provides carbohydrates, Vitamin C and potassium
  • Can be eaten boiled, fried, mashed or processed into chips
  • Creates jobs in farming, transport and processing industries
